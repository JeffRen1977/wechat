Google closed its $32 billion acquisition of cybersecurity company Wiz this week — the biggest acquisition in Google’s history, as well as the largest ever acquisition of a venture-backed startup.

On the latest episode of TechCrunch’s Equity podcast, Rebecca Bellan, Sean O’Kane, and Anthony Ha were joined by Shardul Shah, a partner at Wiz’s largest shareholder Index Ventures. Shah discussed his history with Wiz, which extends before Wiz itself — he previously backed Adallom, the startup previously founded by Wiz’s Assaf Rappaport, Ami Luttwak, and Roy Reznik.

They discussed why the company was such an appealing acquisition target and how Shah responded when Wiz walked away from Google’s previous acquisition offer.

“It’s no surprise that it’s Wiz,” Shah said. “Wiz is at the center of three tailwinds: AI, cloud, and security spend.”

Shah emphasized that Wiz’s leadership team — CEO Assaf Rappaport, co-founders Ami Luttwak, Yinon Costica, and Roy Reznik — created a culture of trust and fast execution. Their platform secured cloud infrastructure and code in production, helping customers prioritize critical threats.

He also noted the wider implications of Google’s acquisition: access to Google’s cloud, AI talent, and resources while maintaining Wiz’s independent culture.

Finally, Shah said the deal inspires entrepreneurs globally and could spark new startups as Wiz employees and investors reinvest their gains. He reflected on the decade-long relationship with the founding team, including the moment when Wiz previously rejected a Google acquisition offer, emphasizing belief in the founders’ judgment.
